<p><strong>The 3D Printing in Jewellery Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Stereolithography (SLA)</li><li>Selective Laser Sintering (SLS)</li><li>Digital Light Processing (DLP)</li><li>Fused Depositing Modelling (FDM)</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The 3D printing in the jewellery market encompasses various technologies. Stereolithography (SLA) uses UV light to cure resin, creating highly detailed designs. Selective Laser Sintering (SLS) employs lasers to fuse powdered material, suitable for creating intricate metal pieces. Digital Light Processing (DLP) also uses light but relies on projectors for faster layer curing. Fused Deposition Modelling (FDM) extrudes thermoplastic filaments, often for prototypes. Other methods include binder jetting and metal 3D printing, expanding design possibilities in the jewellery sector.</p></p>
